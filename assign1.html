What is carbon footprint

A carbon footprint is the total greenhouse gas (GHG) emissions caused by an individual, event, organization, service, place or product, expressed as carbon dioxide equivalent

How Carbon Footprints Work

  1. Carbon footprints measure how much carbon dioxide (CO2) we produce just by going about our daily lives.
  2. When fossil fuels burn, they emit greenhouse gases Like CO2 that contribute to  global warming.
  3. Ninety-eight percent of atmospheric CO2 comes from the combustion of fossil fuels

Global Warming

An increase in Earths temperature has been observed during the past decades, believed to be due to increase of Carbon dioxide(CO2)

Although some production of greenhouse gases is natural, Human activity has increased the production substantially

Reducing Carbon footprint

  1. Driving less
  2. Plant trees
  3. Reduce, reuse, recycle
